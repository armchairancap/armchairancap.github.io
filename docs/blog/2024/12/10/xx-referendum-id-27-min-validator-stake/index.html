xx Network referendum (ID 27) on hiking the minimum validator stake

December 10, 2024 · 3 min read
ArmchairAncap

Introduction​

Recently xx Network held its 28th democracy referendum (referendum ID 27) on the topic of hiking the minimum validator stake to 15,000 XX and subsequently as required (and depending on market conditions and the price of xx token).

Currently it is 1,000 XX which is just $60 - totally laughable!

How did we get here and why this hike was necessary​

Originally the minimum validator stake was much higher (14,000 XX) and that was when the coin was several times more valuable. Now the minimum stake is 1/14 of the amount and 1/4 of the value, which means in fiat terms it's 1/64-th of what it was.

As usual, when it comes to bad decisions it's now unclear how "it was decided" to make the nonsense cut to 1,000 XX and who was/were the main supporter(s) of that when it was done.

But looking forward, it's easy to see that the current minimum validator stake doesn't make any sense.

  • It makes mockery out of NPOS. You can validate by staking $60
  • It's bad for security of the network. You can stand up 200 validators by staking just $12,000
  • It makes scamming easier for bad actors. You can stand up 30 validators with $1,800 and cycle through them and create new ones as you scam nominators on a different node every day
  • It's a tiny fraction of minimum validator stake elsewhere

Unbelievably, we still got a vote against the hike!

Who was against?​

Just 6ZFHpAxagxZV8E2GFyMEJoTgRvEsBgEh7vHMYSu6p7bLwufg.

You can see here or on-chain: 114 in favor and 1 against.

The wallet that voted against may very well be the sockpuppet operator behind this pool.
