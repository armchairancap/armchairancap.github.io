Notes before the change of minimum validator stake on xx Chain

December 31, 2024 · 5 min read
ArmchairAncap
  • What changed, how and why
  • Why is 15K better than 1K
  • What now
  • What has to be done
  • Take-aways

What changed, how and why​

Those who follow xx Network are likely aware of the changes to the minimum validator stake.

Until era 1140 (today), validators could start validating with only 1,000 XX (less than $50).

The intent to introduce changes and "return" to the old limit were first approved in a referendum, and then a separate democracy vote approved the change to chain variables, with a scheduled task set to increase the minimum stake to 15,000 XX. The first referendum also approves additional hikes in 2025.

How it was before:

  • 14,0000 XX
  • When XX token was worth 4-5 times as much

Why was it reduced to 1,000 XX? Because several loud socialists in the community (who don't know anything about economics) made more noise than the rest.

With the new hike the minimum stake is "higher", but only in XX coins. It's still some 80% lower than the already low stake that the network used to have before.

Why is 15K better than 1K​

Because xx Network is as Proof of Stake network.

Validators must have a lot at stake to demonstrate their commitment to good, secure validation. It's nothing new.

The new minimum stake will make it harder (more expensive) to scam nominators. Starting with tomorrow's era it will cost 15 times as much to stand up a bunch of nodes, wait until they're nominated and elected, and then hike commission from the initial "below 20%" standard to 30% or 40% until nominators figure it out. Once they do, you spin up a new node with just $50 on it and start again.

It will also make node hoarding more expensive. You can still run a 20 node validator farm and undermine the security of the network, but for new adversaries that becomes 15 times more expensive.

The hike doesn't "solve" either of these problems, but it makes such behavior more expensive.

What now​

The xx socialists claim 15,000 XX is already unbearable for the "poor validators" who haven't been making profit and now won't be able to afford 14,000 XX to continue validating.

First, that's false, because they can keep going as long as they don't stop validating.

Second, if it's not sustainable, the solution isn't to lower the security of the network.

Tomorrow's Phragment election will be the first one which will prevent new entrants, but all the existing ones will be able to continue.

Of course, the scammers have their little VM-based nodes ready to go: among 24 nodes currently in Waiting, only three seem regulars. I checked all of them and 21 had 1,010 XX or less on them.

They don't have to get elected by tomorrow - they just need to be there and not stop validating before they get elected.

What has to be done​

xx Network suffers from the low price of the token which makes validation unprofitable.

That cannot (or at least: should not) be fixed by compromising the security of the network.

But the community is disengaged (the same problem seen in other DAOs such as Polkadot) and 90% of active community members are either "degens" (price-focused speculators) or socialists, making any improvements difficult.

The xx Foundation and Technical Committee members are staying out of these (seemingly "non-fundamental") governance discussions which is appropriate, but the end result is not satisfactory.

Validator slots should be shrunk, but the groups I mentioned are against because it's (supposedly) "bad PR". By that logic adding 10 more slots should help (after all, there's as many as 24 nodes in waiting!), but they don't suggest that. I wonder why.

Take-aways​

As we are about to enter era 1141, the hike seems entirely justified and obviously helpful - it doesn't need to "solve" anything, it just needs to make the undermining of security more expensive, which it does by a factor of 15.

What else should be done?

  • Validator slots should be cut by 10 or 20 to ensure nodes require at least 300K XX to get elected. Now a handful has less than 100 K XX. The network doesn't need 370 nodes in the first place, so there should be no second thoughts about this and yet here we are with dozens of people running many nodes with 1K XX validator stake on each...
  • A xx Postage bounty should be created and released as soon as ongoing bounties are delivered, to ensure real demand for tokens in second half of 2025. A RFC is on the forum and just needs to be worked on and released.

Aside from that, governance needs to be improved to create more engagement both internally (validators, nominators), but also externally (with builders). Most of that is not happening because of this stalemate between a disengaged majority, a loud (but incompetent) minority of validators, and an unopinionated Foundation. I hope this will be resolved and improved in 2025.
