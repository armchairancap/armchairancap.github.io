How to add WXX (ERC-20) xx coin to Supra StarKey

Introduction​

StarKey wallet is the native wallet for Supra L1.

xx coin is the native token of xx Network, a L1 network with integrated mixnet services for post-quantum privacy.

WXX is an XX Foundation-issued, Ethereum-wrapped ERC20 token. It is also used on Uniswap DEX.

This post explores StarKey for xx coin users (and speculators).

Non-native tokens/coins/NFTs in StarKey​

Supra StarKey is expanding its token support and currently support for 20 or so is natively built-in. What about the smalle ones, such as xx coin?

According to StarKey FAQs:

As of now, StarKey supports a variety of tokens, including all ERC-20 tokens, as well as tokens on the Aptos, Sui, and Solana blockchains

This looks promising. Let's try!

WXX contract​

Go to contract address and copy contract (Token Contract (WITH 9 Decimals) on right-hand side).

I got it from https://etherscan.io/token/0x171120219d3223E008558654ec3254A0F206edb2, but I suggest you get it from Uniswap by yourself.

Get StarKey​

Download StarKey by following links from the official site (www.supra.com) or the StarKey app page.

Currently desktop versions have more features, but I can't think of any that would interfere with WXX.

I used StarKey on Chromium.

Add WXX to StarKey​

warning

Make a backup of your StarKey pass phrase. Like with other non-custodial walles, there's no other way to get access.

The pencil icon is where you toggle display (ON/OFF) of existing tokens. But WXX does not exist, so we need to add it.

Once in Manage Token page, you can toggle other tokens (Solana, etc.), but to add WXX you need to click on the + icon to add a custom token.

This is where the WXX contract is needed:

  • Network: Ethereum
  • Contract: paste the WXX contract address you got earlier

The rest will be auto-populated.

You have added WXX!

Now you can go back to Manage Tokens (the pencil icon). Use the magnifying glass icon to find WXX and click the star to make the token a favorite.

Finally, we can click on WXX to receive or send (ETH is required for transaction fees) WXX.

tip

I encountered a problem with StarKey v2.1.2 in this last step: no coins - built-in or manually added - with zero balance would be displayed.

A workaround I discovered:

  • Click on All Networks at the top
  • Click on Testnet and then again on Mainnets and return to wallet

That makes all zero-balance coins appear in view. They may disappear again, but at least you can copy an addresses to fund it and make it a non-zero balance address, or repeat the workaround to view them again at any time.

Why do all this?​

The display problem is temporary and maybe you won't even notice it.

Why bother in the first place? Several reasons.

Supra DEXes​

WXX in your StarKey will (eventually) be trade-able on Supra DEXes.

Currently, one is online (Dexlyn) and more are coming (Atmos, for example).

But again - why bother?

  • Low cost: Dexlyn's DEX charges 0.3% and transaction fees are negligible
  • Fast: transactions take seconds
  • The first native stablecoin on Supra is Dexlyn's dexUSDC, which is USDC (ERC-20) wrapped on Supra L1 using Dexlyn's Ethereum bridge, so you will be able to
    • Trade xx coin for dexUSDC
    • Exit/enter with WXX and USDC (ERC-20)
info

Dexlyn DEX does not yet support a variety of coins - it just launched last week.

The above will be possible when generic ERC-20 coins/tokens become supported on Dexlyn DEX. You would then add your own pairs (such as dexWXX/dexUSDC).

Alternatively - see below - wait for Supra's own cross-chain support.

As an example of low fees, here's an example of a transaction involving Dexlyn's Ethereum bridge.

On Supra L1 side, the fee was US$0.000. Affordable, right?

As you can see the exact amount is 0.001001 SUPRA ($0.00001742). Transaction fees will go up as L1 gets more busy, but even if they go up 10 times, it'll still be cheap! Did I mention that transactions complete in low single digit seconds?

What else should you know?

Your StarKey WXX can be sent to any wallet that supports it, and private key of the address can be exported if you want to simply import the address to another wallet.

For Dexlyn's DEX, they have instructions how to fund dexUSDC by sending USDC (ERC-20) to their bridge.

The only thing missing is dexWXX-dexUSDC pair and the correspoinding Liquidity Pool.

info

If you want to stay up-to-date, follow Dexlyn and Aptos on X.

  • Dexlyn: https://dexlyn.com/
  • Aptos: https://aptos.dev/

Next key step for Delxyn - in the context of this post - is to add support for arbitrary DIY pairs and pools. To trade WXX on Dexlyn DEX we'd need to wrap it, and trade dexWXX/dexUSDC.

For Aptos, it's to launch.

Later on when arbitrary pairs become available, we should be able to wrap WXX on Supra into something like dexWXX and trade/swap/send/receive natively on Supra L1.

info

In a week or so EVM will be available on Supra testnet.

Technically savvy folks could take the WXX contract (published by the XX Foundation) and release $SXX (Supra-wrapped XX on Supra L1) on Supra testnet.

xx coin and Supra ecosystem​

Supra is just getting started. It's 10 weeks old.

What's coming soon is:

  • Native EVM support (the flagship language is Move, and MoveVM is the first VM supported since TGE), which means the Ethereum Smart Contract used to issue WXX could be issued on Supra L1 without using Ethereum L1
  • Bridgeless (yes, bridgeless) cross-chain swapping on Supra. When a Substate chain (probably Polkadot or Kusama) becomes supported, xx Network could be supported as well, but xx Network would have to update chain to support latest and greatest Substrate SDK features

Either of these would be a huge improvement over WXX.

In the first case, with an SdexXX (assuming this be the name of a xx ERC-20 token created on Supra L1 using the same contract that was used for WXX) it would take seconds to exit to native xx tokens. And it'd cost almost nothing. Sound good?

In the second case, WXX would become redundant and xx Network wouldn't even need a bridge.

SupraNS​

Another interesting thing is SupraNS, the official Supra name system, i.e. "Supra's ENS". It's here and requires Supra tokens to register. It's much cheaper than ENS, which is great, but because it just launched there's no wallet that supports it.

info

StarKey will likely be the first to support SupraNS.

With SupraNS support in StarKey it will be easier to send tokens to Supra wallet.

Later on, when SupraNS allows for more entries (such as URLs and maybe arbitrary fields), we may be able to add things like nicknames from xx Network Haven (the reference quantum-resistant messaging app).

How to buy Supra​

note

This is not a recommendation or advice.

If you don't want to use Dexlyn and only want to try WXX in StarKey, you don't even need Supra. You just need WXX and $ETH for transaction fees. This will use Ethereum L1 so it won't be particularly interesting, exciting, fast, or cheap.

If you want to play with Supra tokens, SupraNS or Dexlyn DEX, today you need just 2 dollars.

Registering a SupraNS name now takes 1-40 SUPRA (depending on name length) and transaction fees are low as shown above.

If you want to play with this stuff (e.g. SupraNS), you need to fund your StarKey with either $USDC (ERC-20; add it through Manage Tokens). You can use $USDC to swap to dexUSDC and/or $SUPRA. Or you can use just $SUPRA.

  • $USDC to $SUPRA - Dexlyn has instructions on how to fund $dexUSDC with $USDC over their Ethereum bridge. This is possible, but slightly complicated. I'd just buy $SUPRA and send it to my StarKey.
  • Supra tokens get be swapped for dexUSDC on Dexlyn DEX at https://app.dexlyn.com/
  • Native $SUPRA - buy it on MEXC (most xx users have an account there) and withdraw it to your StarKey
    • You can also get $SUPRA from Houdini, SimpleSwap, and few other CEX (see CoinMarketCap or CoinGecko)
note

If you want to try out existing dexUSDC-Supra Liquidity Pool, you need to stake it with more than $2, so I don't recommend this.

That's no longer playing, but speculating/investing or whatever you want to call it.

Once you have $SUPRA you can register SupraFS names and buy/sell $dexUSDC. You can also pay for Supra L1 transaction fees.

Next steps​

That's not all! Supra has quite a few other tricks up their sleeve! Follow SupraLabs on X to see what they're up to.

If you're a Supra user interested in privacy, you can follow xx Network to learn how StarKey and dApps could use xx Networks' mixnet (cmix) to provide privacy for its users./

info

xx Network's Proxxy can be modified to support Supra L1 and route all client RPC calls through its mixnet, adding seconds of latency, but increasing privacy of the user.

Good use cases for StarKey and Proxxy: regular wallet transactions and anything else that can tolerate an extra 2-3 seconds required to route (encrypted) Supra RPC client calls through xx Network's cMix mixnet.
