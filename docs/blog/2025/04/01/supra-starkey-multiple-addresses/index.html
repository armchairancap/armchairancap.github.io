Multiple addresses in Supra StarKey wallet

Introduction​

Supra Name Service (SNS) is one of my favorite features on the Supra Network.

This post shares some tips related to using multiple wallets and addresses, as well as SNS, in StarKey wallet.

Use multiple addresses​

Why? Because wallets can get drained.

Secondly, although Supra isn't a privacy-focused network, multiple addreses can be better for your privacy as well.

Use multiple wallets​

Should you use multiple wallets?

Maybe yes. If your wallet password gets compromised, all addresses in it are at risk.

How could a wallet be exposed and not all its addresses get drained?​

I've mentioned wallets and addresses can get drained, but how could just one address in a wallet get drained?

Because when you connect to a dApp, you pick an address - not all addresses - from your wallet.

If malware that auto-approves transactions takes over, it may approve transactions, but may not necessarily go through the entire list of addresses, connect and drain them all.

If malware is any good, it'll drain all addresses, but if not, some may survive.

Wallet setup​

Given the above, you can consider your risks and convenience.

I like a setup like this: two wallets, several addresses.

WalletAddressPurposeSNS
1TreasuryStaking(none)
1LPLP-ing(none)
2PlayTrusted dAppsmyname.supra
2TestingTesting untrusted dAppstest.myname.supra

I wouldn't even give SNS names to addresses (accounts) in wallet One.

For wallet Two, I think it's nice to have a name at least for "frquent use" address. You could have two names (one or both subdomains) or two unrelated names (e.g. play.supra, test.supra), but sending tokens between them would identify them as likely owned by the same user in any case.

tip

As the first wallet (Treasury/LP) is most valuable, it's best to not even use it on the same device where your wallet Two is used.

Furthermore, you may want to buy a Ledger hardware wallet for that wallet. Wallet number two may not be valuable enough to warrant protection (especially if the lack of convenience bothers you).

Add addresses to same StarKey wallet​

This step is not very obvious, but it's simple enough. Click on the round cricle in top left corner.

The next step requires some thinking.

Let's say I've created my wallet 2 and in it I already have my "Play" address. I want another one for Testing.

In order of my personal preference:

  1. Sub-account "Testing" will be recoverable with the same recovery method - it's a "cousin" of your Play account

  2. New account - different recovery, but approximately same risks, so I don't like this approach

  3. Imported account - this is the least convenient method, as you need to create an account (address) elsewhere and get its private key.

Imported accounts​

These seem like a hassle, but they have some advantages as well.

Disadvantages:

  • Just getting a private key out of a wallet and sending/copying it around is risky and may result in a compromised address (not wallet)
  • You need to protect that private key ... somehow, because StarKey can't recover it for you. So you'll also need to keep it encrypted (or printed on paper) somewhere

Advantages:

  • This method allows you to create and use "vanity address", such as 0xb00b5... (i.e. "boobs"). In fact, you can create and import several such addresses, and it's easy to find them and recognize them in SupraScan even without a name
  • If you intend to use the same address from the CLI or similar, you'll need to have a copy of your private key elsewhere anyway

My use cases for imported accounts are related to both of the items listed under advantages (you can read about it in a recent post here).

Basic privacy protection​

This is actually one of the main reasons for this post: as I've mentioned Supra transacations are pseudo-anonymous, but let's do what we can.

That is, make at least one hop between wallets One and Two, so that the link between wallet Two and One is not obvious.

Method one​

warning
  • Some CEX reuse addresses, so doing this multiple times doesn't work well privacy-wise
  • It is recommended to deposit and withdraw different amounts
  • Don't withdraw from the same CEX to both wallet One and Two

  1. Send Supra from wallet One to a CEX

  2. Withdraw (different amount) funds from CEX to wallet Two

Method two​

Send ERC20 USDC (Ethereum) from a CEX to Metamask.

From there, send USDC to Dexlyn bridge to get dexUSDC, then use Dexlyn Swap to swap dexUSDC to Supra using an address in wallet Two.

Like in Method One, you may want to swap from dexUSDC to Supra in random-sized chunks over a period spanning at least several days.

Method three​

You could also buy Supra on a CEX and send to wallet Two, as presumably you'll never need a lot of Supra for addresses in wallet Two.

But - see Method number One - if you do it multiple times or send from CEX to both wallet One and Two, it's downhill from there.

--

No method gives you Monero-like privacy (far from that!), but that's okay: our sole purpose is to not make it obvious to scammers that you also own wallet One.

Furthermore, if your wallet One is better secured (e.g. separate device, Ledger, multi-sig), it's even not that critical.

In any case, sooner or later you'll make a mistake and send from wallet One to wallet Two or vice versa, so don't sweat it - just secure wallet One and you'll be fine.

Take-away​

Multiple wallets are useful for obvious reasons. High-value wallets should not be installed on the same device as low-value, frequent-use wallets.

It's also advisable to use multiple accounts within each. I prefer to use sub-accounts, but other approaches may work for certain use cases.

For "frequent use" wallets SupraNS names are convenient and any approach (domains, subdomains, etc) is fine for most users.
