Taking a break from xx Network

April 12, 2025 · 7 min read
ArmchairAncap
  • Introduction
  • Issues
    • Community (mis)management
  • Where are xx whales?
  • Next steps

Introduction​

I'm not in the mood to make a detailed post, but I suppose I should push myself to make it longer than a tweet.

After all, I've spent years validating on xx Network and committed non-insignificant resources in it.

Issues​

I've written about all of them here, e.g. about validator centralization, poor community governance that led to that, and more.

In short, democracy ruined it for me.

When I started validating, I didn't know almost anything about Polkadot-style DAOs because I'd been out of crypto for years and prior to that I was involved in Bitcoin ecosystem which didn't have fancy DAO-style governance features.

Even after 1-2 years of validating on xx Network, I didn't understand that the project effectively belonged to the community. By the time I started disliking it and recognized the problem it was too late.

The Foundation has been far from helpful, but xx coin holders made decisions along the way, so I don't know what the Foundation could have done even if its members were competent. The community has displayed nothing but utter incompetence and the Foundation helped them turn some of it into reality.

The Council was similar: no role or power to do anything but "guide", but also no conviction to raise alarms and stop the community from running the project into the ground.

The jeet lundatics have been fully in chartge of running the asylum.

Those aren't the only problems, but they are the key problems.

Community (mis)management​

the community votes on all changes to the technology and network. ~ https://xxfoundation.org/

In theory, that's great. In practice, if you get bad voters you also get bad voting outcomes.

Early on, validator selection wasn't strict enough. Perhaps they thought they'd need "X validators" to be able to operate at the scale that was envisioned, or they wanted to be "inclusive" and recruit all sorts of people from as many geos as possible.

Then, despite the fact that validators were too many, rather than kicking out disengaged validators they kept all of them while handing out excessively generous handouts during testnet.

Over 90% of validators don't even participate in the community and many run multiple nodes. Validators and holders who see all this are disappointed and stop voting.

Jeets, sociopaths and other trash takes over the community. The new priority is "marketing", i.e. anything that pumps the price with the Foundation's money and enables orderly exit at a profit.

In theory, xx whales should be interested in this as well, but seeing they'd have to deal with a community dominated by trashy elements, they decide to write off their investment and move on to managing investments that can be managed.

The Foundation isn't loaded to be able to do what the "community" wants and probably can't do it anyway (legal and governance reasons), so the craziest ideas get a priori rejected. But little good happens either and certainly no overhaul of the validator system.

By now most good holders are either completely out (like me) or completely disengaged (i.e. don't bother to try to impact the agenda, let alone vote), the whales don't participate so their votes are missing, while trashy validators and pools keep running their small and large farms and gaining voting power over the last remnants of sanity in the community.

Where are xx whales?​

I don't know. It seems Roger Ver may be one, but he's been occupied lately.

If the rest of the whales had any sense, they would have ganged up and taken over because DAO votes always gather just a small fraction of all votes.

Even after 5 years of validating I've never learned of any whale I've never spoken to one and, even as I've tried to advocate certain improvements during 2023 and 2024, not one ever reached out to me.

From that I conclude they either don't bother to keep an eye on the project because the coin dropped a lot soon after it launchyed or they had ideas different from mine. (I suspect it's not the latter because I've never seen anything sensible apart from Richard's development bounties and hackathons.)

For example, I wanted to stop large centralized pools, implement improvements to on-chain identity module (in xx Network Wallet) and focus on postage development. The first was rejected, the second too, and the third for some reason never got out of a discussion on draft implementation (see xx Network forum for more, although I've blogged on some of those issues here).

The jeets and scammers in the community want stupid marketing campaigns, more CEX listings and similar nonsense (including having the Foundation pay for "market makers" (read about the MM scam here)).

The idea is to get the Foundation use its money to create enough liquidity or hype to facilitate the scammers' selling of xx coins earned running pools of validator nodes.

The Foundation didn't go ahead with the worst parts of it (maybe some of it was simply due to the lack of funds rather than the desire to do the right thing), but plenty of time and money has been wasted on nonsense community proposals and at the same time the Foundation wouldn't do anyhthing but rubber-stamp what they could.

note

Even the delayed 2025 roadmap released this month doesn't have anything on postage!

That should have been at the top of their list, and yet another year will go buy without a single use case for xx coin!

In the case that didn't register: after years of mainnet, there isn't one product or service that xx coin holders can buy!

With that kind of state of affairs what happens in the long term is the community harms the project. The Foundation may stop or delay funding for some very bad or expensive ideas here and there, but in the long term the outcome is the same.

note

The Foundation has done a lousy job. We would have been much better off if Richard was in charge of the project as its benevolent dictator.

I'd like to thank Richard and Keith for help and guidance over the years.

I should probably add that leaving Aaron out doesn't mean he's not good. I'm more interested in the tech side of xx Network, so I've rarely communicated with Aaron and really don't know what - if anything - he could have done differently. And when I say "the Foundation" I don't mean Aaron - I mean the people who never show up and do absolutely anything of use.

Next steps​

There are no next steps, really.

warning

I've powered off and deleted all nodes. I will disable wallets and stop nominating as well.

I may move Haven documentation to a sub-section of this site, which will probably break some links.

Sorry in advance for any inconvenience.

I didn't become a validator because I needed the income (I've only ever sunk resources into this project), but because I wanted to participate in the community and build a privacy focused network.

Likewise, I didn't quit because I got rich validating, but because participating hasn't worked for me for years. You fix one window, the NPCs break two.

I hope some smart whales gang up and take over this failed "democracy" and the DAO itself and turn things around so I'll keep an eye on xx Network on X. I'm skeptical about the chances of that happening because so far I've seen nothing and it should have happened years ago.

Other than that, I don't plan to continue actively participating in the xx Network community.

By now it's very hard to undo the damage created by years of mismanagement and hands-off DAOism. The community jeets and validator pool scammers are noisy, vote regularly and have earned enough coins to negatively impact democracy propposals when only 2-3% of all holders vote. At the same time there's no mechanism to expropriate them or claw back coins they earned from handouts and network-harming validation pools. It's probably unfixable.
